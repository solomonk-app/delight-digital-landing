# 05 · Reusable Script Template (30–45s)

Every daily script uses this six-beat spine. Target **80–110 spoken words**. Short sentences for voiceover. On-screen text **supports**, it does not duplicate, every spoken word.

---

## The six beats

| Beat | Seconds | Job | Rule |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 · Hook | 0–2s | Stop the scroll | One line. A feeling or a question. No brand yet. |
| 2 · Relatable problem | 2–8s | "That's me" | Name the beginner moment honestly. No shame. |
| 3 · One useful takeaway | 8–18s | Teach one thing | Exactly one idea. Plain words. |
| 4 · Simple demo/example | 18–32s | Show it | One real prompt / one result. Keep data out. |
| 5 · Natural bundle bridge | 32–38s | Connect, softly | The bundle is "where this goes next," not a hard pitch. |
| 6 · CTA | 38–45s | One clear ask | Rotate CTAs (see `06`). Educational videos may skip the product CTA. |

**On-screen text layers:** (a) a title card at 0–2s, (b) monospace step/prompt cards during beats 3–4, (c) a fixed end card during beats 5–6.

---

## Worked example (Day 2 — vague vs. improved prompt · ~95 words)

> **[Hook]** "If AI keeps giving you boring answers, it's probably not you — it's the question."
> **[Problem]** "Most of us type one line — 'plan a workout' — and get something generic back."
> **[Takeaway]** "Good prompts have five little parts: who it should act as, the task, who the answer's for, the format, and the tone."
> **[Demo]** "Watch: 'Act as a gentle physiotherapist. A 15-minute morning workout for someone at a desk with a sore lower back. Numbered list, one rest day, warm and reassuring.' Same tool — completely different answer."
> **[Bridge]** "That five-part recipe is the first thing we practice in the workbook."
> **[CTA]** "Save this and try it on something today."

On-screen text: title "VAGUE vs. USEFUL" → two prompt cards (bad/good, ingredients highlighted) → end card + "link in bio."

## Fields every `scripts/day-XX.md` must contain
- Video objective · Target viewer · Pillar & format
- Final voiceover (word-counted)
- Scene-by-scene visual plan with timestamps
- Exact on-screen text
- B-roll / screen-recording instructions
- Instagram caption · TikTok caption · YouTube Shorts title + description
- CTA (which one) · Search keywords · Hashtags
- Bundle component featured · Safety/verify notes

---

## Writing rules
- One idea per video. If you're explaining two things, it's two videos.
- Say "a tool like ChatGPT or Claude," not a price or a model version.
- Every example prompt keeps personal data as `[brackets]`.
- Any medical/legal/financial example is framed as **preparation for a professional**, never advice.
- Close relatable/fear videos on capability, not anxiety.
