## Final voiceover (92 words)
"Every good prompt is basically a recipe with five parts. Let me show you with a thank-you note. Vague version: 'Write a thank-you note.' You'll get something stiff and generic. Now the recipe. Role: 'Act as a warm, down-to-earth writer.' Task: 'Write a short thank-you note for a [gift someone sent].' Audience: 'It's for my [aunt].' Format: 'Three or four sentences.' Tone: 'Warm and genuine, not formal.' Same tool, completely different note — one that actually sounds like you. Role, task, audience, format, tone. Learn those five, and you can prompt anything."
