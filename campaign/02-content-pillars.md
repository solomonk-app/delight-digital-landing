# 02 · Content Pillars

Five pillars carry the whole campaign. Each video belongs to exactly one, and the mix produces the target funnel balance.

---

## The five pillars

| # | Pillar | Share of videos | Job to be done | Primary metrics |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Beginner AI education** | ~35% | Teach one small, usable skill | Saves, follows |
| 2 | **Relatable frustrations & fears** | ~25% | "That's me" recognition; build trust | Comments, shares |
| 3 | **Immediate-use demonstrations** | ~25% | Give one prompt to try today | Saves, shares |
| 4 | **Product / interactive-workbook demos** | ~10% | Show the real thing works | Profile visits, clicks, sales |
| 5 | **Trust, privacy, safety + gentle promotion** | ~5% | Reassure; soft CTA | Saves, trust, clicks |

**Rolls up to the required funnel balance:**
- **~60% educational / immediately useful** = Pillars 1 + 3
- **~25% relatable / trust-building** = Pillar 2
- **~15% direct product promotion** = Pillars 4 + 5 (promo half of 5)

Across the 30-video calendar: Pillar 1 ≈ 6 videos, Pillar 2 ≈ 7, Pillar 3 ≈ 11, Pillar 4 ≈ 4, Pillar 5 ≈ 2 (≈60/25/15 once Pillar 3's "try it" demos count as educational-useful).

---

## What each pillar sounds like (guardrails)

- **1 · Education.** One idea per video. Plain English. Name a tool only generically. End with a save-prompt. *Never* a mini-lecture with jargon.
- **2 · Relatable.** Name the feeling ("everyone else seems to get it"), then relieve it — never amplify shame or fear. Always leave the viewer feeling *more* capable, not less.
- **3 · Demonstrations.** Show one real prompt and one real (or realistic) result. Keep personal data out of examples. "First answer is a draft — ask it to try again."
- **4 · Product demos.** Screen-record the actual sites. Only claim verified features (saves locally, copy buttons, certificate, 120 prompts). No income/transformation claims.
- **5 · Trust/safety.** Reassure without scaring. "Here's the simple habit that keeps you safe," not "AI will steal your identity." Pair every safety point with a calm fix.

## Banned in every pillar (hard rules)

- No exaggerated income or transformation claims ("make $X," "change your life").
- No fear-based messaging, fake urgency, or fake scarcity.
- No aggressive sales language, no "tech bro" tone, no jargon.
- No generic "AI will change everything."
- Never portray adults 35–60 (or older) as incapable of learning tech.
- Never use a child's health/safety or a caregiver's stress as a pressure tactic.
- Never present AI as a doctor, therapist, lawyer, or financial advisor — it's preparation for a professional conversation.
- No unverified tool facts (prices, models). Teach evergreen habits.
