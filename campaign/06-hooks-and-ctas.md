# 06 · Hooks, CTAs, Captions, Titles & Keywords

Never reuse the same hook or CTA on consecutive videos. Match the tone to the calm brand — curiosity and reassurance, never hype or fear.

---

## 12 hook formulas (fill the blanks)

1. **The permission hook:** "You're not behind on AI. You're just beginning."
2. **The blank-box hook:** "If you've opened ChatGPT and just stared at the empty box — this is for you."
3. **It's-not-you hook:** "If AI keeps giving you boring answers, it's not you — it's the question."
4. **One-task hook:** "You don't need to learn all of AI. Just one useful task today."
5. **Overwhelm hook:** "There are dozens of AI tools. You need exactly one to start."
6. **Myth-flip hook:** "Everyone says AI is complicated. Here's the whole skill in 20 seconds."
7. **Relatable-scene hook:** "It's 5 o'clock, the fridge is random, and no one's planned dinner…"
8. **Fear-to-calm hook:** "Worried AI will get things wrong? Good — here's the 5-second habit that keeps you safe."
9. **Question hook:** "What's the one thing you'd ask AI if you knew it wouldn't judge you?"
10. **Before/after hook:** "Same question, two ways — watch the answer completely change."
11. **For-you hook (segment):** "If you're a parent / caregiver, this one's for you."
12. **Gentle-confession hook:** "I used to think AI wasn't for people like me. Here's what changed."

## Caption template (reusable)

```
[Hook line — one sentence, matches the video's first line]

[2–3 short lines: the takeaway in plain words, or the prompt written out
so people can copy it from the caption.]

[Soft CTA — one line, rotated from the bank above.]

[Gentle disclaimer when relevant: "AI can be wrong — always double-check
anything important. Not medical/legal/financial advice."]

.
.
[hashtags]
```

- **Instagram:** 1–3 lines above the fold + the prompt; end with save/link CTA.
- **TikTok:** slightly more conversational; put a searchable phrase in the first line.
- **YouTube Shorts:** short caption; the *title* does the work (below).

---

## YouTube Shorts title formula

`[Beginner outcome] + [plain qualifier]` — written as something people search.
- "How to write a better AI prompt (for total beginners)"
- "Which AI should I use? A simple beginner's guide"
- "ChatGPT for beginners: what to type first"
- "How to spot a scam text with AI (5-second check)"
- "AI for parents: the prompt I use every week"

Keep it under ~60 characters where possible; front-load the keyword.

## Evergreen-safety note for all copy
Name tools generically. Don't quote prices or model versions in captions/titles unless verified that week. Every safety/verify claim must pair with a calm fix, never a scare.
