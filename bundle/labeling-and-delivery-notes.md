# Bundle Labeling & Delivery Notes

Fixes to make the paid bundle read clearly and honestly. These are recommendations + a clean manifest; some require re-rendering PDFs (a design step).

---

## 1. Remove "FREE" from the in-bundle Quick-Start Guide
- **Issue:** `Delight Digital — AI Free Beginner's Guide.pdf` is titled/labeled "A FREE BEGINNER'S GUIDE" and calls the interactive version "free" on its closing pages. Shipping a "FREE"-branded file *inside a paid bundle* can make a buyer feel they paid for something advertised as free.
- **Fix:** produce an in-bundle edition titled **"AI Made Friendly — Quick-Start Guide"** (drop "Free"). Keep the actual free lead-magnet copy (with "Free") for MailerLite only. Same content, two labels for two contexts.
- **Position it** as a "15-minute quick start," never as the main justification for the price.

## 2. Soften "BONUS" on core-value packs
- **Issue:** 155 Parent Prompts ("BONUS"), 18 Caregiver Prompts ("A BONUS PACK"), and the Permission Slip ("A BONUS") are labeled bonuses. Calling the bulk of a paid product "bonuses" undercuts perceived core value.
- **Fix:** relabel as **"Included"** / **"Part of the bundle"** on covers and on the sales page. Reserve "bonus" language for at most one genuine extra (e.g., the Permission Slip).

## 3. Fix the free-guide prompt count
- **Issue:** the 16-page guide's cover/TOC promises **"24 ready-made prompts"** but only **15** are shown (3 per page × 5 pages). The closing page also says "120+" online while other bundle assets tout "155."
- **Fix:** either add the missing 9 prompts or change the cover to the true count (e.g., "15 ready-made prompts"). Make the online numbers consistent (the `ai.` site shows 120; the parent pack is 155 — keep those distinct and clearly labeled).

## 4. Absorb the 52-page edition (do not double-ship)
- Replace `Not-Behind-Just-Beginning Bonus.pdf` in the customer package with the slim **Quick Reference & Tool Guide** (see `quick-reference-tool-guide.md`). Never deliver two "editions" of *Not Behind, Just Beginning*.

## 5. Design consistency (minor)
- The Caregiver pack and Permission Slip use a slightly wider-spaced serif than the tighter display face in the other PDFs. On next re-render, unify to one serif for a seamless set. Non-urgent.
